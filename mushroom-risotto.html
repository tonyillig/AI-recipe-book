Mushroom Risotto

Mushroom Stock · Mixed Fungi · Parmesan · Properly Stirred

Mushroom risotto done well is one of the great vegetarian main courses — deeply savoury, creamy without being heavy, built on a mushroom stock that amplifies every layer of fungi flavour. The technique is the same as scallop risotto: keep the stock warm, add it ladle by ladle, stir consistently, and finish with butter and Parmesan off the heat. The dried porcini — soaked in hot water and used to enrich the cooking liquid — are the secret weapon.

01 Ingredients
  • 320garborio or carnaroli rice
  • 500gmixed fresh mushrooms — chestnut, shiitake, oyster, portobello
  • 30gdried porcini mushrooms soaked in 400ml boiling water — that liquid becomes stock
  • 1.5lvegetable or mushroom stock, kept warm throughout
  • 200mldry white wine
  • 4 largeshallots, finely diced
  • 4 clovesgarlic, minced
  • 80gunsalted butter, cold, cubed — divided
  • 80gParmesan, finely grated
  • 6 sprigsfresh thyme
02 The Method
1

Rehydrate the Porcini and Build Your Stock

Soak 30g dried porcini in 400ml boiling water for 20 minutes. Lift out the mushrooms, squeeze gently, and roughly chop. Add the soaking liquid to your stock — strain it first through a fine sieve to remove grit. Keep the combined stock warm in a separate pan throughout. Cold stock kills the risotto process.

The porcini soaking liquid is the secret weapon of this dish. It's intensely flavoured mushroom stock. Don't discard it — it goes straight into the risotto pot via the ladle.
2

Sauté the Fresh Mushrooms

Heat olive oil in a wide pan over high heat until shimmering. Add 500g fresh mushrooms in a single layer — in batches if needed — and cook without moving for 3–4 minutes until deeply golden. Season, toss briefly, and cook another 2 minutes. Remove and set aside.

Mushrooms need high heat, enough space, and to be left alone. Crowding them means they steam and go grey and release all their water. Hot pan, space, no movement. Golden mushrooms are the goal.
3

Build the Risotto Base

In the same pan, melt 40g butter over medium heat. Add the shallots and thyme and cook for 8 minutes until deeply soft. Add garlic and rehydrated porcini and cook 2 more minutes. Add 320g rice and stir for 2 minutes. Pour in 200ml wine and stir until fully absorbed.

4

Add Stock Gradually — 18 to 20 Minutes

Add the warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring gently between each addition and waiting until each ladle is absorbed before adding the next. This takes 18–20 minutes total. The rice should be al dente. Remove from heat. Stir in the remaining 40g cold butter and 80g Parmesan vigorously off the heat — this is the mantecatura and it creates the creamy texture. Fold in three-quarters of the sautéed mushrooms. Season. Top with reserved mushrooms and a drizzle of truffle oil if using.

The mantecatura happens off the heat. If you do it on the heat, the butter splits and you get a greasy dish. Off the heat, cold butter, vigorous stirring. That sequence.

Deeply Mushroom. Properly Creamy. 🍄

You soaked the porcini and used the liquid. You sautéed the fresh mushrooms at high heat. You kept the stock warm and added it gradually. You finished off the heat with cold butter and Parmesan. This is mushroom risotto done correctly.
