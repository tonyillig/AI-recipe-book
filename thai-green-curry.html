Thai Green Curry

Homemade Paste · Coconut Milk · Tofu & Vegetables · Vegan

There are two kinds of Thai green curry: the kind made with a jar of paste, and the kind made from scratch where you blend green chillies, lemongrass, ginger, garlic, and a handful of aromatics into a paste that smells like a Thai market and tastes like the reason paste-in-a-jar exists as a category (because this is so much better). This version is fully vegan, built around tofu and vegetables, and does not compromise on flavour for a single second. The homemade paste is the move. It always will be.

01 Ingredients
The Green Paste
  • 4–6green chillies Thai bird's eye for heat, long green for mild — or a mix
  • 2 stalkslemongrass, white part only, roughly chopped
  • 30gfresh ginger, peeled
  • 4 clovesgarlic
  • 3shallots, roughly chopped
  • 1 bunchfresh coriander, roots and stems included the roots are the flavour; don't discard them
  • 4kaffir lime leaves, stems removed
  • 1 tsp eachground cumin, ground coriander
  • 1 tspsalt
The Curry
  • 400gfirm tofu, pressed and cubed press for at least 30 min; the drier it is the better it fries
  • 2courgettes, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 smallaubergine, cubed
  • 150gsugar snap peas
  • 2 cansfull-fat coconut milk full-fat only — light coconut milk makes a thin, sad curry
  • 200mlvegetable stock
  • 2 tbspcoconut oil
  • 2 tbspsoy sauce or tamari tamari to keep it GF
  • 1 tbsppalm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2limes, juiced
  • 1 bunchThai basil, to finish
02 The Cook
1

Make the Green Paste

Add all paste ingredients to a food processor or high-speed blender. Blend until as smooth as possible — add a small splash of water if needed to get things moving. The finished paste should be vivid green, deeply fragrant, and slightly chunky. Taste it: it should be bold, citrusy, and intensely herbal. If it needs more salt, add it here.

The smell of fresh green curry paste being made is one of the most vibrant, alive smells in all of cooking. It smells green. It smells like lemongrass and chilli and coriander root all at once. It is the exact opposite of opening a jar of paste and it is completely worth the 5 extra minutes.
2

Fry the Tofu Until Golden

Press the tofu dry with paper towels. Heat 1 tbsp coconut oil in a wide pan over high heat. Fry the tofu cubes without moving them for 3–4 minutes per side until golden and crispy. Season lightly with salt. Remove and set aside. This step is optional but transforms tofu from passive filler to something that actually contributes texture to the dish.

Pressing tofu is the difference between tofu that fries golden and tofu that just sits there steaming in the pan. Wrap it in a clean towel, put a heavy book on top, wait 30 minutes. That moisture needs to leave before any oil touches it.
3

Bloom the Paste in Coconut Cream

In a large wok or wide pan, heat the coconut cream from the top of one can over medium-high heat. Let it cook for 2–3 minutes until it starts to split and sizzle. Add the green paste and fry in the coconut cream for 3–4 minutes, stirring constantly. This is the most important flavour-development step — frying the paste in fat blooms the aromatics and drives off the raw chilli and garlic edge.

The moment the green paste hits the hot coconut cream is the moment your kitchen starts smelling like a restaurant in Bangkok and everyone within earshot knows something serious is happening. This is a smell that converts people.
4

Build and Simmer

Add the remaining coconut milk and the vegetable stock. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle simmer. Add the aubergine and courgette and cook for 8–10 minutes until just tender. Add the sugar snap peas, tofu, soy sauce, and sugar. Simmer for 3 more minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning — it should be a balance of creamy, spicy, salty, sweet, and sour. Finish with lime juice and Thai basil leaves. Serve over jasmine rice.

Do not boil vigorously once the coconut milk is in — it will split and lose its creaminess. A gentle simmer is the correct temperature for the whole sauce stage.

From Scratch. Better in Every Way. 🌿

You made the paste. You fried the tofu properly. You bloomed the paste in coconut cream. You built a curry that has depth and heat and freshness and that jar on the shelf simply cannot replicate. Fully vegan. Completely satisfying. This is the one.
