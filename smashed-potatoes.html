Crispy Smashed Potatoes

Boiled · Smashed Flat · Roasted Until Shattering · Herbs & Salt

Smashed potatoes require the most restraint at the table. You boil small potatoes until completely tender, then smash them flat, then roast in plenty of oil at high heat until the edges shatter. The texture is the thing — maximum surface area in contact with the hot, oily pan means maximum crust, which means maximum contrast between the crunchy exterior and the fluffy interior. Objectively better than regular roast potatoes and less prep.

01 Ingredients
  • 800gbaby potatoes or small waxy potatoes
  • 60mlolive oil — more than you think you need is the correct amount
  • 2 tspkosher salt, for boiling water
  • 4 clovesgarlic, unpeeled — added to the tray
  • 4 sprigsfresh rosemary or thyme
  • to finishflaky sea salt, lemon juice, extra herbs
02 The Method
1

Boil Until Completely Tender

Place 800g potatoes in well-salted cold water. Bring to a boil and cook for 15–20 minutes until completely tender — a knife should slide in with zero resistance. They need to be softer than you think. Drain and steam dry for 5 minutes.

2

Smash Them Flat

Place the potatoes on a large, oiled baking tray with space between each one. Using the bottom of a glass, press each potato flat — about 1cm thick with rough, craggy edges. The rougher the better. Drizzle generously with 60ml olive oil.

The oil needs to get under the smashed surface and around all those craggy bits. Do not be conservative. The oil is what makes them shattering. Oil up.
3

Roast at High Heat Until Shattering

Preheat oven to 220°C / 425°F. Season with salt. Add garlic cloves and herb sprigs to the tray. Roast for 35–40 minutes, turning the tray halfway but not flipping the potatoes — let them build their crust undisturbed. Done when the flat surfaces are deep golden and shattering at the edges. Finish with flaky salt and a squeeze of lemon.

Smashed potatoes do not look like much going into the oven. They look extremely correct coming out. This is the glow-up arc of root vegetables and it happens every single time.

Boiled. Smashed. Shattered. 🥔

Maximum surface area, maximum crust, minimal effort. Vegan, GF, completely irresistible. Make them alongside anything and they will be gone first.
