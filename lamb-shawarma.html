Lamb Shoulder Shawarma

Overnight Spice Rub · Slow Roasted · Garlic Sauce · Flatbread

Shawarma is not a recipe. It is a commitment to the overnight marinade and the long, patient roast. The spice paste soaks into the lamb for a full night, and then the shoulder slow-roasts until it collapses into shreds that are simultaneously crispy-edged and impossibly tender inside. Pile it into flatbread with toum (the aggressively good Lebanese garlic sauce), pickled chillies, and fresh parsley. This is the dish your guests will still be talking about two weeks later.

01 Ingredients
The Lamb & Spice Paste
  • 2kgbone-in lamb shoulder
  • 2 tsp eachground cumin, ground coriander
  • 1 tbspsmoked paprika
  • 1 tsp eachturmeric, cinnamon
  • ½ tsp eachallspice, cayenne
  • 6 clovesgarlic, minced to a paste
  • 60mlfresh lemon juice
  • 60mlolive oil
  • 2 tspkosher salt
The Garlic Sauce (Toum)
  • 4 clovesgarlic
  • 1 tspsalt
  • 200gGreek yoghurt or make full toum with neutral oil — both are correct
  • 1 tbsplemon juice
To Serve
  • 6flatbreads or pitta
  • 1 bunchfresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • 1 jarpickled chillies or pepperoncini
  • to servesliced tomatoes, cucumber, sumac onions
02 The Cook
1

Make the Spice Paste and Marinate Overnight

Combine all the spices, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and salt into a rough paste. Score the lamb shoulder deeply all over — several 2cm cuts through the fat cap and into the flesh. Rub the paste aggressively into every surface and deep into the cuts. Wrap tightly in clingfilm and refrigerate for a minimum of 8 hours, ideally overnight. The longer it marinates, the deeper the flavour penetrates.

Scoring and pasting a lamb shoulder is the most meditative task in all of cooking. You're getting the spice paste into the actual muscle. This is the whole game. Take your time. Be thorough. You will taste the difference.
2

Slow Roast at Low Heat

Take the lamb out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Place in a deep roasting tray. Add a splash of water to the bottom of the tray to prevent burning. Cover tightly with foil and roast at 160°C / 325°F for 4 hours. Remove the foil for the final 30–40 minutes and raise the temperature to 220°C / 425°F to crisp the exterior.

You're looking for the bone to wiggle freely and the meat to shred with minimal resistance. If it's not there at 4 hours, give it another 30 minutes covered and check again.
3

Make the Garlic Sauce

While the lamb rests, blend 4 garlic cloves with a pinch of salt into a paste. Stir through the Greek yoghurt and lemon juice. Season. This is your garlic sauce — quick, simple, and exactly right for this dish. For a more traditional toum, emulsify garlic into neutral oil with a bit of lemon — same principle as aioli but more aggressive about the garlic situation.

4

Shred, Pile, and Eat

Rest the lamb for 15 minutes, then shred with two forks — you want a mix of crispy exterior pieces and tender interior. Warm the flatbreads. Spread garlic sauce generously. Pile on the lamb. Top with parsley, pickled chillies, and whatever else you like. Roll or fold and eat immediately.

The crispy edges mixed with the soft middle mixed with the cold garlic sauce mixed with the pickled chilli is a flavour and texture combination that should be illegal. It is not illegal. Enjoy it fully.

The Real One. 🥙

You marinated it overnight, roasted it low and slow, and caramelised the edges at the end. The garlic sauce took four minutes. The flatbread was warm. This is shawarma done properly at home, and it is absolutely worth every hour of the overnight wait.
