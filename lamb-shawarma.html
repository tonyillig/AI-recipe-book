Lamb Shoulder Shawarma

Overnight Spice Rub · Slow Roasted · Garlic Sauce · Flatbread

Shawarma is not a recipe. It is a commitment to the overnight marinade and the long, patient roast. The spice paste soaks into the lamb for a full night, and then the shoulder slow-roasts until it collapses into shreds that are simultaneously crispy-edged and impossibly tender inside. Pile it into flatbread with garlic sauce, pickled chillies, and fresh parsley. This is the dish your guests will still be talking about two weeks later.

01 Ingredients
The Lamb & Spice Paste
  • 2kgbone-in lamb shoulder
  • 2 tsp eachground cumin, ground coriander
  • 1 tbspsmoked paprika
  • 1 tsp eachturmeric, cinnamon
  • ½ tsp eachallspice, cayenne
  • 6 clovesgarlic, minced to a paste
  • 60mlfresh lemon juice
  • 60mlolive oil
  • 2 tspkosher salt
Garlic Sauce (Quick Version)
  • 4 clovesgarlic
  • 200gGreek yoghurt
  • 1 tbsplemon juice, salt to taste
To Serve
  • 6flatbreads or pitta
  • 1 bunchfresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • 1 jarpickled chillies or pepperoncini
02 The Cook
1

Make the Spice Paste and Marinate Overnight

Combine all spices, garlic, 60ml lemon juice, 60ml olive oil, and salt into a paste. Score the lamb shoulder deeply all over — several 2cm cuts through the fat cap and into the flesh. Rub the paste aggressively into every surface and deep into the cuts. Wrap tightly and refrigerate for a minimum of 8 hours, ideally overnight.

Scoring and pasting a lamb shoulder is the most meditative task in all of cooking. You're getting the spice paste into the actual muscle. Take your time. Be thorough. You will taste the difference.
2

Slow Roast at Low Heat

Take the lamb out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Place in a deep roasting tray with a splash of water in the bottom. Cover tightly with foil and roast at 160°C / 325°F. The lamb is ready to unwrap when a probe in the thickest part of the shoulder reads 88–93°C / 190–200°F — roughly 4 hours, but go by temperature not time. Remove the foil, raise to 220°C / 425°F, and roast until the exterior is deeply caramelised and crisped — about 30–40 minutes more.

Look for the bone to wiggle freely and the meat to shred with minimal resistance. The probe temp of 88–93°C is your target — bone movement and easy shredding confirm it. If the probe reads lower, give it 30 more minutes covered and check again.
3

Make the Garlic Sauce, Shred, and Serve

Blend 4 garlic cloves with a pinch of salt into a paste. Stir through 200g Greek yoghurt and a tablespoon of lemon juice. Rest the lamb 15 minutes, then shred with two forks. Warm the flatbreads, spread garlic sauce generously, pile on the lamb, top with parsley and pickled chillies.

The crispy edges mixed with the soft middle mixed with the cold garlic sauce mixed with the pickled chilli is a flavour and texture combination that should be illegal. It is not illegal. Enjoy it fully.

The Real One. 🥙

You marinated it overnight, roasted it low and slow, and caramelised the edges at the end. The garlic sauce took four minutes. The flatbread was warm. This is shawarma done properly at home.
