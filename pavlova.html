Pavlova

Crisp Shell · Marshmallow Centre · Whipped Cream · Fresh Fruit

Pavlova is the dessert that looks the most dramatic for the least technical effort. The meringue base — crunchy shell, soft marshmallow centre — takes 15 minutes to prepare and then the oven does the work while you exist. Topped with softly whipped cream and as much fresh fruit as you can pile on, it is simultaneously the most impressive and the most forgiving thing you can put on a dessert table. Cracks in the meringue are not failures; they are places for the cream to pool. This recipe is on your side.

01 Ingredients
The Meringue
  • 6large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 300gcaster sugar
  • 1 tspwhite wine vinegar or lemon juice — creates the marshmallow centre
  • 1 tspcornflour same job as the vinegar — both give you that soft, chewy middle
  • 1 tspvanilla extract
The Topping
  • 400mlheavy cream, cold
  • 2 tbspicing sugar
  • 400gfresh mixed berries — strawberries, raspberries, blueberries
  • 4passion fruit, pulp only
02 The Method
1

Whip the Meringue to Stiff Peaks

Ensure your bowl and whisk are completely clean and grease-free — a single drop of yolk or fat will prevent the whites from whipping. Beat the egg whites on medium speed until soft peaks form. Add the caster sugar one tablespoon at a time, waiting about 10 seconds between each addition. Once all the sugar is in, increase to high and beat until the meringue is glossy, thick, and holds stiff peaks — when you lift the whisk, the peak should stand straight up without drooping. Fold in the vinegar, cornflour, and vanilla by hand.

Adding the sugar too fast causes a grainy, weeping meringue. One tablespoon at a time, properly incorporated each time. Patience here is the whole technique.
2

Shape and Bake

Draw a 24cm circle on baking parchment and flip it (pencil-side down) onto a baking tray. Pile the meringue onto the circle and shape it with a spatula — build up the sides slightly higher than the centre so it forms a natural well for the cream. Bake at 120°C / 250°F for 1 hour 15 minutes. Turn the oven off and leave the pavlova inside with the door closed for at least 1 hour — or leave it overnight. The slow cool prevents cracking.

The pavlova base can be made 2 days ahead and kept in a cool, dry place. Do not refrigerate — the humidity softens the shell. Top with cream and fruit no more than 1 hour before serving.
3

Whip the Cream and Top

Whip the cold cream with icing sugar to soft peaks — you want it holding its shape but not stiff. Pile it generously into the centre of the pavlova. Top with fresh berries and spoon the passion fruit pulp over everything. The passion fruit is not optional — its tartness is exactly what the sweet cream and meringue need. Serve immediately once topped.

A fully loaded pavlova on a table is one of the most visually correct things that can exist at a dinner party. It is a showpiece. It is also made of egg whites and sugar and cost almost nothing. The ratio of impact to cost is genuinely unhinged.

Cloud. Cream. Fruit. The Holy Trinity. 🍓

Crisp outside, marshmallow inside, billows of cold cream, fresh fruit piled on top. This is one of the most beloved desserts in the world for reasons that become completely obvious the first time you eat a good one. You made a good one. It cracks? That's character. The cream pools in the cracks. It's better that way.
