Sourdough Focaccia

Overnight Cold Ferment · Dimpled & Oiled · Flaky Salt · Rosemary

Focaccia is the most forgiving bread you can make and one of the most rewarding. The overnight cold ferment does all the work — you mix a loose, sticky dough, refrigerate overnight, and it develops flavour and structure on its own while you sleep. The next day you dimple it aggressively into an oiled pan, let it relax for an hour, and bake until the bottom is golden and crispy, the top is glistening with flaky salt, and the interior is impossibly airy and pillowy.

01 Ingredients
  • 500gstrong bread flour all-purpose works but bread flour gives better structure
  • 400mlroom temperature water
  • 7 g (1 sachet)instant yeast
  • 10gkosher salt
  • 50mlolive oil, into the dough
  • 80mlgood olive oil — for the pan and the top
  • 2 tbspflaky sea salt — for the surface
  • 4 sprigsfresh rosemary, leaves stripped
02 The Method
1

Mix the Dough — No Kneading Required

Combine 500g flour, yeast, and 10g salt in a large bowl. Add 400ml water and 50ml olive oil. Mix until a shaggy, sticky dough forms — no dry patches. Do not knead. Cover with cling film and refrigerate overnight, or up to 18 hours.

The dough should look wet and sticky and slightly worrying. This is correct. Wet dough = open, airy crumb. Trust the overnight process. It always works.
2

Oil the Pan and Stretch the Dough

Pour most of the 80ml surface olive oil into a 23×33cm (9×13 inch) baking tray. Remove the dough from the fridge and tip it into the tray. Use oiled hands to gently stretch it towards the corners. It will spring back — let it rest 30 minutes, then stretch again. Leave for another 30 minutes.

3

Dimple Aggressively

Using all ten fingers, press dimples all the way through the dough to the bottom of the tray. Do not be tentative about this. Deep, aggressive dimples. Drizzle the remaining olive oil over the top. Scatter rosemary leaves and flaky salt generously.

The dimpling step is the most fun you will have baking. You get to aggressively press your fingers into dough for 30 seconds and it is not only allowed but mandatory. This recipe wants you to be decisive with it.
4

Bake Hot — 22–25 Minutes

Preheat oven to 220°C / 425°F. Bake for 22–25 minutes until the top is golden, the edges are pulling away from the tray, and the bottom is deep golden and crispy. Rest 5 minutes, then lift out onto a wire rack. Eat warm.

The bottom must be golden, not just the top. Carefully lift one corner with a spatula to check. That crispy oily bottom is the reward for all the olive oil.

Dimpled. Oiled. Salted. Glorious. 🫒

You made bread. Without kneading. With an overnight ferment that did all the work. Golden on top, crispy on the bottom, pillowy in the middle, fragrant with rosemary and good olive oil. This is the bread you'll make every weekend once you know how easy it is.
