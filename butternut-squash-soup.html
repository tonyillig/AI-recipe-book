Butternut Squash Soup

Roasted Squash · Brown Butter · Toasted Seeds · Deeply Savoury

There is a version of butternut squash soup that is sweet and slightly cloying and tastes like dessert in a bowl. This is not that version. This version roasts the squash hard until it caramelises and takes on colour, builds a base of onion, garlic, and ginger, finishes with brown butter for depth, and is deliberately savoury — seasoned aggressively, tempered with smoked paprika, topped with toasted seeds and crispy sage. It is a weeknight soup that tastes like a restaurant intended it.

01 Ingredients
  • 2 largebutternut squash (~2kg total), halved lengthways, seeds scooped out
  • 3 tbspolive oil
  • 2yellow onions, diced
  • 6 clovesgarlic
  • 30gfresh ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1 tsp eachground cumin, smoked paprika
  • 1 litregood vegetable stock
  • 60gunsalted butter use olive oil to keep it vegan — still excellent
  • 60gpumpkin seeds — for toasting
  • 6 sprigsfresh sage or thyme
  • to finishcrème fraîche or swirl of coconut cream, flaky salt
02 The Method
1

Roast the Squash Hard

Halve the squash lengthways and scoop out the seeds. Brush the cut surfaces with olive oil, season with salt and pepper. Place cut-side down on a baking tray. Roast at 210°C / 410°F for 40–45 minutes until completely tender and the cut face has caramelised and taken on deep golden-brown colour. This caramelisation is where the depth comes from — pale, steamed squash is not the same dish.

Placing them cut-side down is the key. The cut surface sits directly on the hot tray and caramelises. Cut-side up gives you a steam bath instead.
2

Build the Base

While the squash roasts, cook the onions in olive oil over medium heat for 12–15 minutes until deeply soft and golden — take your time here. Add the garlic, ginger, cumin, and smoked paprika and cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant. When the squash is done, scoop the flesh from the skin and add it to the pot. Pour in the stock.

3

Blend Until Silky

Use a stick blender (or transfer in batches to a blender) and blend until completely smooth and silky. Season assertively with salt — butternut squash needs more salt than you think. Thin with a splash more stock if needed. Keep warm over low heat.

When blending hot liquid in a standing blender, fill it no more than halfway, hold the lid down with a folded towel, and start on the lowest speed. Hot steam builds pressure and lid-off incidents are real and unpleasant.
4

Brown Butter, Toast Seeds, Finish

In a small pan, melt the butter over medium heat and cook until it turns golden brown and smells nutty — about 4 minutes. Watch it constantly; it goes from brown to burnt quickly. Remove from heat. In a separate dry pan, toast the pumpkin seeds with a pinch of salt until they start to pop. Ladle the soup into bowls, swirl in brown butter (or olive oil), add a spoon of crème fraîche, scatter pumpkin seeds, and finish with flaky salt and fresh thyme.

Brown butter on butternut squash soup is the finishing move that makes people ask "what is in this" because they can't identify it but know it's correct. It smells like hazelnuts and tastes like everything is more itself. It takes 4 minutes. Always do it.

Savoury. Silky. Roasted All the Way Through. 🎃

You roasted the squash cut-side down until it caramelised. You built a properly cooked onion base. You browned the butter. The difference between this and the versions that taste like dessert is every single one of those decisions. This is the soup that converts people who thought they didn't like butternut squash soup.
