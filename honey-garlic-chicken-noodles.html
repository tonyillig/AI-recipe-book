Honey Garlic Slow Cooker Chicken & Noodles

The vibe: You throw 680g of chicken breasts into a slow cooker. You pour a glossy, salty-sweet honey-garlic situation on top. Four hours later you walk back into the kitchen and your house smells like a Korean fried chicken shop merged with a Sunday roast. You shred. You thicken. You boil noodles. You toss. You eat something that has absolutely no business tasting this rich for the amount of effort it required. This is the takeout you don't have to order.

01 The Doctrine

Slow cookers were built for exactly this kind of recipe — a sweet-savoury liquid, a protein that benefits from time, and a finishing move that pulls everything together at the end. The chicken cooks in the sauce, not just under it, so every shred carries flavour all the way through. The noodles get cooked separately so they don't turn to soup, then drink up the thickened sauce in the last five minutes. Simple. Loud. Correct.

The original version of this recipe leans heavy on honey and goes light on the finishing touches. We're keeping the bones identical — same slow cooker, same core sauce — but adding fresh ginger, a toasted-sesame finish, and a real green onion situation at the end. Same effort. Significantly better dinner.
02 The Ingredients
The Chicken
  • 680gboneless skinless chicken breasts — thighs also work and are arguably more forgiving; the slow cooker is kind to both
  • ½ tspkosher salt
  • ¼ tspblack pepper, freshly ground
The Sauce
  • 120mlsoy sauce — low sodium if you have it; regular is fine if you don't add the optional finishing salt
  • 120mlhoney — a runny, basic honey works perfectly; don't waste the fancy stuff here
  • 60mlwater — for the braising liquid
  • 5 clovesgarlic, finely minced — pre-minced jarred garlic also works; we don't gatekeep
  • 1 tbsptoasted sesame oil — the dark, toasted kind; not the pale neutral one
  • 1 tbspfresh ginger, grated — upgrade from ground; the original recipe used dry, fresh is significantly better
  • 1 tbsprice vinegar — optional but it cuts through the sweet and makes the sauce taste alive
The Thickener & The Noodles
  • 2 tbspcornstarch
  • 60mlcold water — stays separate from the braising water; this is for the slurry only
  • 450gwide egg noodles — the thick, ribboned kind; fresh udon also works and is a serious move
The Finish
  • 4green onions, thinly sliced — whites and greens kept separate; whites go in the sauce, greens go on top
  • 1 tbsptoasted sesame seeds — for garnish; toast them in a dry pan for 60 seconds until fragrant
  • optionalred pepper flakes or chili crisp — if you want heat to cut the sweet; highly recommended
03 Build the Pot
1

Season and Place the Chicken

Pat the 680g chicken breasts dry with paper towels and season both sides with the kosher salt and pepper. Place them in a single layer in the bottom of your slow cooker. If they overlap a little, that's fine — they'll release liquid and even out as they cook. Do not stack them three deep. Spread them out as much as your insert allows.

Wet chicken into a slow cooker is fine, but seasoned, dried chicken into a slow cooker is lowkey unhinged behaviour because nobody ever does it. Salt before braising is a free flavour upgrade. The chicken thanks you. We're doing the small thing.
2

Whisk the Sauce

In a measuring jug or medium bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, water, minced garlic, sesame oil, grated fresh ginger, and rice vinegar if using. Also add the white parts of the sliced green onions. Whisk for 20 seconds until the honey has loosened up and everything looks unified. The mixture should be glossy, deep amber, and smell unreasonably good already.

If your honey is solid or crystallised, microwave it in a heatproof bowl for 15–20 seconds to loosen before whisking. Cold honey will sink to the bottom of the slow cooker and burn against the wall over 4 hours, which is a tragic fate for honey.
3

Pour and Cover

Pour the sauce evenly over the chicken. Tilt the slow cooker insert side to side to make sure the sauce gets underneath and around — you want the chicken sitting in liquid, not just under a glossy puddle. Cover with the lid. Cook on LOW for 4 hours. If you only have time for HIGH, 2 to 2.5 hours works but the texture isn't quite as silky.

Resist the urge to lift the lid and check. Every time you open a slow cooker, it loses heat that takes 20+ minutes to recover, which adds real time to your cook. Trust the machine. Walk away. Live your life.
04 Finish the Sauce
4

Check for Tenderness — 4 Hours In

The chicken is done when a fork pressed into the thickest part meets almost no resistance and the meat starts to flake apart on its own. Internal temp should read at least 74°C / 165°F. If you push a fork in and the chicken still feels firm and springy, give it another 20–30 minutes. Chicken breast in a slow cooker is forgiving — but only up to a point. Past 5 hours on low it starts to dry out even submerged.

5

Shred Inside the Pot

Using two forks, shred the chicken directly in the slow cooker. No need to remove it to a board — every juice that drips off would just go back in anyway. Shred into bite-sized pieces, not threads; you want the texture to hold up against the noodles. Stir the shredded chicken through the sauce so every piece gets coated.

The shredded-chicken-in-glossy-sauce moment is one of the most satisfying things you can do in a kitchen with almost no effort. It looks like food styling. It is food styling. You did this. With one bowl and a small appliance.
6

Slurry the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk the 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with the 60ml of cold water until completely smooth — no lumps. This is your slurry. Pour it into the slow cooker and stir through the chicken and sauce. Turn the slow cooker to HIGH and leave the lid off. Stir every couple of minutes for the next 8–10 minutes. The sauce will go from thin and watery to thick, glossy, and clinging to the back of a spoon.

Cornstarch needs to be mixed into cold water before adding to hot sauce. Adding dry cornstarch straight into hot liquid creates instant lumps that will not whisk out, no matter how hard you try. Slurry first. Always.
The moment the sauce transitions from "soup" to "sauce" is real and dramatic and happens in about 90 seconds. You'll see it. The spoon will start dragging tracks through the pot. That's the sign. Science is undefeated.
05 The Noodles
7

Boil the Egg Noodles Separately

While the sauce is thickening, bring a large pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil. Add the 450g egg noodles and cook according to package directions — usually 6–8 minutes for wide egg noodles. Cook them to just barely al dente — they'll soak up more sauce after, and overcooked noodles in a sauce situation is a sad outcome.

Reserve about 120ml of the starchy noodle water before you drain. If the final dish gets too thick or claggy, a splash of this loosens everything back up and makes the sauce coat better. Pasta water rules apply here too.

8

Drain and Combine

Drain the noodles in a colander — do not rinse them. The surface starch helps the sauce grip. Transfer the drained noodles directly into the slow cooker with the chicken and sauce. Using tongs or two big spoons, toss everything together until every noodle is coated in glossy sauce. If the sauce seems too thick or the noodles are clumpy, add a splash of the reserved noodle water and toss again.

9

Rest for 5 Minutes — Yes, Really

Put the lid back on and turn the slow cooker to WARM or OFF. Let everything sit for 5 full minutes. This is the move that makes the dish. The noodles drink up the sauce, the flavours marry, and the texture goes from "freshly tossed" to "deeply combined." Skipping this step is the difference between dinner and great dinner.

Five minutes feels like nothing but it is everything. The noodles are doing real work here. They're absorbing. They're becoming. We respect the process. We do not rush the noodles.
10

Finish and Serve

Lift the lid. Scatter the green tops of the green onions and the toasted sesame seeds generously over the top. If you're going for heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a spoonful of chili crisp. Serve directly from the slow cooker into wide bowls. Hit each bowl with another small pinch of sesame seeds and green onion because presentation matters when something looks this good.

Heat Move
A spoonful of chili crisp on top transforms this dish completely. Sweet, salty, glossy noodles + crunchy spicy oil = restaurant-tier bowl. Strongly suggested.
The Veg Add
Steamed broccolini, snap peas, or quick-blanched bok choy on the side keeps the dish from feeling one-note. A handful of raw shredded carrot stirred in at the end also slaps.
The Lunch Pivot
Refrigerates beautifully for 3 days. Reheat with a splash of water or stock to loosen the sauce. Genuinely better cold the next day with chopsticks straight from the container — that's the no-cap honest take.
Storage
Keeps for 3 days in the fridge. The noodles soak up sauce as they sit, so add a splash of liquid when reheating. Freezes poorly — the noodle texture suffers. Make it, eat it within the week.
