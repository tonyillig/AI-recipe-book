Tomato Tart

Rough Puff Pastry · Herbed Ricotta · Heirloom Tomatoes · Summer

This tart exists because of what happens to a good tomato in a hot oven. Heirloom tomatoes, placed cut-side up on herbed ricotta, on rough puff pastry, at high heat: the tomatoes collapse, concentrate, caramelise at the edges, and release their juice into the ricotta below, which sets into something creamy and rich and deeply flavoured. The rough puff pastry shatters and puffs and holds the whole thing. Eat warm, with the best olive oil you have. This is the summer dish.

01 Ingredients
The Rough Puff Pastry
  • 250gplain flour
  • 180gcold unsalted butter, cut into 2cm cubes — keep in the freezer until needed
  • 80mlice-cold water, added gradually
  • 1 tspsalt
The Herbed Ricotta
  • 250gricotta
  • 40gParmesan, finely grated
  • 1 clovegarlic, minced to a paste
  • handfulfresh basil, finely chopped
  • 4 sprigsfresh thyme, leaves stripped
  • 1lemon, zested
The Tomatoes
  • 600gheirloom tomatoes, sliced 1cm thick a mix of colours and shapes is both beautiful and delicious
  • 3 tbspolive oil
  • flaky saltfor the tomatoes before roasting
02 The Method
1

Make the Rough Puff

Combine 250g flour and salt. Add 180g frozen butter cubes. Using your fingertips (working quickly), break the butter into irregular, flat, pea-sized pieces — not fully rubbed in. You want visible butter flakes. Add 80ml ice water gradually and mix until the dough just comes together — rough and shaggy. Flatten into a rectangle, wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

The visible butter flakes in rough puff are not a mistake — they are the whole point. Those flakes create steam in the oven that puffs and separates the layers.
2

Roll, Fold, Chill

On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled dough into a long rectangle. Fold the bottom third up and the top third down. Turn 90 degrees, roll out again, fold again. Wrap and refrigerate for another 20 minutes. This quick two-fold process creates the layers.

3

Make the Ricotta, Salt the Tomatoes

Mix 250g ricotta, 40g Parmesan, garlic, basil, thyme, lemon zest, salt, and pepper until combined. Taste — herby, rich, well seasoned. Slice 600g tomatoes, place on paper towels, season with salt. Let sit 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture. Pat dry. This step prevents a soggy-bottomed tart.

Salting the tomatoes to draw out moisture is the difference between a crispy-bottomed tart and one that's gone soggy from tomato water. Salt them. Pat them. Proceed.
4

Assemble and Bake

Roll the chilled pastry to roughly 30×40cm. Transfer to a lined baking tray. Score a 2cm border all the way around — don't cut through. Spread the ricotta inside the border. Arrange the tomato slices over the ricotta. Drizzle with olive oil. Brush the border with beaten egg. Bake at 200°C / 400°F for 30–35 minutes until the pastry is deeply golden, the border has puffed, and the tomatoes are collapsed and caramelised. Rest 5 minutes, scatter fresh basil and flaky salt.

The scored border is the frame that allows the pastry edges to rise while the filling stays put. Don't put toppings over the border — the egg wash needs a clear surface to puff and brown properly.

Pastry. Ricotta. Tomatoes. Summer. 🍅

You made rough puff. You herbed the ricotta. You salted the tomatoes. You watched the border rise in the oven and the tomatoes collapse into the cream. Make this in tomato season with good tomatoes and it will taste extraordinary.
