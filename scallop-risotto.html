Scallop Risotto

Okay bestie, no cap — this is the most bougie thing you will ever cook, and it's actually not that hard. Risotto is just rice that needs your attention. Think of it like a Tamagotchi: ignore it and it dies. Stay with it, keep stirring, and you'll have something that absolutely slaps. Your family will think you went to culinary school. 01 Ingredients
The Main Characters
  • 680gfrozen bay scallops (40–60 per lb) — thaw in fridge overnight or in cold water for 30 min
  • 280gArborio rice — must be Arborio, regular rice won't slay the same
  • 1200mlseafood or chicken broth, warmed — low sodium preferred
  • 180mldry white wine — Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc; sub extra broth if needed
The Supporting Cast (still iconic)
  • 3 largeshallots, finely diced — shallots are milder and sweeter than onion, perfect for delicate seafood
  • 4 clovesgarlic, minced — fresh is the vibe; jarred is a last resort

  • 30mlolive oil, divided
  • 100gfreshly grated Parmesan — grate it yourself, pre-shredded is lowkey mid
  • 30mlfresh lemon juice — about 1 lemon; bottled is not it
  • 1 tsplemon zest
  • handfulfresh parsley, chopped
  • to tastekosher salt and black pepper
  • pinchred pepper flakes — optional but it adds rizz
Equipment You'll Need
  • 1 largewide, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven — risotto headquarters

02 Let's Cook (No Skipping Steps, frl)
1

Thaw & Prep the Scallops

If you didn't thaw the scallops overnight in the fridge, place them in a zip-lock bag and run cold water over them for 20–30 minutes. Never use warm water — that's cooked fish before you even start. Once thawed, dump them onto a plate lined with two layers of paper towels, press gently, and let sit for 10–15 minutes. The drier they are, the better they'll cook. Pat them again right before they hit the pan.

Wet scallops = sad, gray, steamed scallops. Dry scallops = golden, caramelized, absolutely sending. We want sending.
2

Warm the Broth

Pour 1200ml broth into the medium saucepan and set it over low heat. You want it warm — not boiling, not cold, just quietly steaming. Keep it on the back burner the entire time you're making the risotto. Adding cold broth to risotto shocks the rice, messes with the starch release, and makes the whole texture go off. Warm broth = smooth, creamy risotto. This is not a drill.

Keep a ladle right in the broth pot so you're not fumbling for it every 2 minutes. Future you says thanks.
3

Build the Flavour Base (The Soffritto Era)

In your large, heavy pot, heat 15g butter and 15ml olive oil over medium heat. Once the butter melts and stops foaming, add your finely diced shallots. Cook for 4–5 minutes until soft, translucent, and fragrant — you're not browning them, you're softening them. Low and slow is the main character energy here.

Once the shallots are soft, add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes if using. Stir constantly for about 60 seconds. Garlic burns fast — literally in under a minute on high heat — and burnt garlic tastes bitter and will ghost your entire dish's flavour.

The smell at this point? Absolutely bussin. Your whole house is about to smell incredible. That's the flex.
4

Toast the Rice (Yes, You Toast It)

Add 280g Arborio rice directly into the pot. Stir around so every grain gets coated in the butter and oil. Keep stirring over medium heat for 2 minutes. The rice will go from chalky white to slightly translucent at the edges — that's the toast. Toasting the rice builds a little armour around each grain so it cooks evenly and stays al dente instead of turning to mush. This step is non-negotiable.

5

Deglaze with Wine (The Fancy Part)

Pour in 180ml white wine all at once. It will sizzle loudly — that's normal and also extremely dramatic, just how we like it. Stir constantly until the wine is almost completely absorbed by the rice, about 2–3 minutes. The wine adds acidity and depth that makes the finished dish taste complex and restaurant-worthy.

That sizzle sound is the rice saying "I'm that girl." Respect it.
6

The Stirring Arc (This Is the Main Quest)

Now begins the most important part: adding broth, one ladle at a time. Add one ladle of warm broth (about 120ml) to the rice and stir. Keep stirring — not frantic, just consistent — until the broth is almost completely absorbed. Then add another ladle and repeat. This process takes 20–25 minutes total.

You don't have to stir like a maniac every second, but stir often — every 20–30 seconds at minimum. This friction releases the starch from the Arborio rice, which is what makes risotto creamy without any actual cream. Science is undefeated.

Taste the rice around the 18-minute mark. It should be just barely firm in the very centre — called al dente. If it's crunchy, keep going. If it's mushy, you went too far.
Risotto is literally a relationship. It needs your time, your presence, and consistent effort. You can't just dump it and dip. Stay in your lane and stir.
7

The Mantecatura (The Big Finish)

When the rice is al dente and the mixture looks creamy and thick, remove the pot from the heat. Immediately add the remaining 30g cold butter, all 100g Parmesan, 30ml lemon juice, and the lemon zest. Stir vigorously for 1–2 minutes. The cold butter emulsifies into the rice and creates that glossy, silky, luxurious texture. Season generously with salt and pepper.

This moment? It's the character development. Everything before this was the origin story. Now we're in the glow-up era.
8

Sear the Scallops (The Grand Finale)

Heat your large skillet over medium-high heat until very hot — give it 2 full minutes to preheat. Add 15ml olive oil. Right before the oil starts to smoke, add the pat-dry scallops in a single layer. Do NOT crowd them — cook in two batches if needed. Let them cook without touching them for 2 minutes. Then toss for another 1–2 minutes. They're done when opaque and golden. Season with salt and pepper.

Bay scallops don't get a hard sear like sea scallops — they're too small. Keep the pan hot and don't overthink it.
Those tiny golden scallops on creamy white risotto? The visual is immaculate. Main character plating energy incoming.
9

Plate It Up (Serve ASAP)

Risotto waits for no one — it firms up quickly as it cools. Serve immediately. Spoon a generous mound of risotto into wide, shallow bowls. Top with a portion of the seared scallops. Scatter fresh chopped parsley over the top. Optionally, a thin slice of lemon on the side and a tiny drizzle of good olive oil. If it looks like something you'd post, it's ready.

A warm bowl. Creamy risotto. Little golden scallops. A sprinkle of green. This is a certified banger. Photograph it. You earned it. Fr fr.
