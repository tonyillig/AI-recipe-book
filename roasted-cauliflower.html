Roasted Cauliflower with Tahini & Pomegranate

A whole roasted cauliflower is one of the most dramatic and least difficult things you can put on a table. The head goes in whole, coated in a spiced oil, and roasts for an hour until it is deeply golden and caramelised all over, tender to the centre, and fragrant with cumin and paprika. The tahini sauce is made in five minutes. The pomegranate seeds are scattered cold over the warm cauliflower and they look extraordinary. This is a vegan dish that commands the centre of a dinner table without apology and satisfies in the same way that a properly cooked piece of meat does. It is one of those recipes.

01 Ingredients
The Cauliflower
  • 1 largewhole cauliflower head, outer leaves removed
  • 4 tbspolive oil
  • 1 tsp eachground cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder
  • ½ tspturmeric
  • ¼ tspcinnamon
  • 1 tspsalt
The Tahini Sauce
  • 100ggood tahini Al Arz or Soom — brand quality matters here
  • 2 lemonsjuiced — roughly 60ml
  • 1 clovegarlic, minced to a paste
  • 60mlcold water — added gradually to achieve pouring consistency
  • pinchsalt
The Finish
  • 1 largepomegranate, seeds extracted
  • 1 bunchfresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • small bunchfresh mint, leaves only
  • 40gpine nuts or flaked almonds, toasted
02 The Method
1

Make the Spiced Oil and Coat the Cauliflower

Mix the olive oil with all the spices and salt into a paste. Trim the base of the cauliflower so it sits flat, but keep the head intact. Rub the spiced oil all over the outside — into every crevice, over the entire surface. Place in a snug roasting dish. Cover with foil and roast at 200°C / 400°F for 40 minutes, then remove the foil and roast for a further 20–25 minutes until the surface is deeply golden and a knife inserted in the centre meets no resistance.

The foil-on phase steams the cauliflower from inside while the outside begins to colour. The foil-off phase is where the caramelisation happens. Both stages are necessary for a cauliflower that is cooked through and golden outside simultaneously.
2

Make the Tahini Sauce

Combine tahini, lemon juice, garlic paste, and a pinch of salt. Whisk — it will seize up and become very thick. Add the cold water gradually, whisking constantly, until you reach a smooth, pourable consistency like double cream. Taste: it should be nutty, tangy, and well seasoned. This takes 3 minutes and is always worth making from scratch.

The tahini seizing up when you add the lemon juice looks alarming every single time — the smooth paste becomes a thick, grainy, seemingly ruined mess. Keep whisking and adding cold water and it smooths out into something silky and perfect. It always does. Keep going.
3

Plate and Finish

Transfer the whole roasted cauliflower to a serving plate or board. Drizzle the tahini sauce generously over and around it — let it pool at the base. Scatter the pomegranate seeds over the top and around the base. Add the fresh parsley, mint, and toasted nuts. Serve whole at the table and let people help themselves — carving it there is part of the drama.

Bringing a whole golden, spiced cauliflower to the table, already drizzled with tahini and scattered with pomegranate seeds, is one of the most visually impactful things you can do with a vegetable that costs under £2. The table goes quiet for a moment. That's the correct reaction.

A Whole Head. A Full Table. Zero Compromise. 🌸

One cauliflower. A spiced oil. An hour in the oven. A five-minute tahini sauce. Pomegranate and herbs scattered cold over the warm surface. This is a vegan dish that takes the centre of the table and holds it, that satisfies completely, and that looks extraordinary with almost no effort. Make it once and you'll understand why it's in the book.
