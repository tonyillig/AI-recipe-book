Sticky Toffee Pudding

Coffee-Soaked Dates · Beef Suet · Fallow-Style Toffee Sauce

▼

Here's the thing about Sticky Toffee Pudding: it has absolutely zero business being this good. Dates. Coffee. Beef fat. A brown sugar sauce with a splash of soy sauce. On paper, that reads like a fever dream. In your mouth, it tastes like the best dessert you've ever had in a countryside pub on a rainy Tuesday in England, which is the highest honor a dessert can achieve. This is the Fallow restaurant version — some unhinged but deeply intentional chef upgrades are baked in. We stop at "you'll be thinking about this for weeks." No cap.

01 Ingredients
The Sponge — Main Characters
  • 250gpitted Medjool dates, roughly chopped — Medjool over regular dried dates; fudgier and more caramel-forward
  • 240mlstrong brewed coffee, hot — not boiling; deepens the date flavor without tasting like coffee
  • 1½ tspbaking soda — goes into the hot date mixture; makes the sponge light
  • 150gself-raising flour — or 150g all-purpose flour + 1½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tspflaky sea salt — Maldon or similar; texture matters
  • 150gdark brown sugar, packed
  • 3large eggs, room temperature
  • 1½ tspvanilla bean paste
  • 100gbeef suet — traditional British fat; lighter, flakier crumb than butter ↑ upgrade
The Sponge — Secret Weapons
  • 1 tspground cinnamon
  • ½ tspground cardamom — makes people say "what IS that?" in the best way
  • 15mlblack treacle or blackstrap molasses — adds gorgeous mahogany color
The Toffee Sauce
  • 200mlheavy cream — double cream if you're in the UK
  • 150gdark brown sugar, packed
  • 50gunsalted butter, cubed and cold
  • 15mllight soy sauce — the Fallow move; adds depth, not soy flavor
  • 1 tspvanilla bean paste
  • pinchflaky sea salt — finish the sauce with this; changes everything
02 Equipment
  • 19×13 inch (23×33 cm) baking dish, well buttered — or individual ramekins for fine-dining vibes
  • 1 largeheatproof bowl — for the date soak
  • 2mixing bowls (one medium, one large)
  • 1medium saucepan — for the toffee sauce
  • 1whisk + spatula, fork, skewer or toothpick
Want the full fine-dining presentation? Divide the batter into 8 buttered 6-oz ramekins. Bake at 175°C / 350°F for 22–26 minutes. Run a knife around the edge and invert onto warm plates. That's the move that makes people gasp.
03 The Sponge (No Skipping, frl)
1

Soak the Dates in Hot Coffee

Combine the chopped Medjool dates with hot strong brewed coffee. Let sit for about 2 minutes, then stir in the baking soda. It'll foam up slightly and turn a deeper, darker color — that's what you want. Let the whole thing rest for 15 minutes. After 15 minutes, mash lightly with a fork until thick and coarse — some texture should remain. Do not purée. Do not use a blender. The little chunks of date in the finished sponge are where the flavor lives.

The hot coffee + baking soda foam-up is the most dramatic thing that happens in this recipe and it's not even the main event. Bestie the date mixture is just getting ready. It's in its prep arc.
2

Mix the Dry Ingredients

Whisk together the self-raising flour, flaky sea salt, cinnamon, and cardamom. The spices go with the flour — distributing them through the dry ingredients means they'll be evenly dispersed throughout the sponge. Set aside.

The cardamom and cinnamon are new additions to the base recipe. They're subtle — no one will say "this tastes like cinnamon." They'll just say it tastes somehow warmer and more complex than any sticky toffee pudding they've had before.
3

Build the Wet Mix

Whisk together the dark brown sugar, room-temperature eggs, vanilla bean paste, and the black treacle or molasses. Whisk for about 1 minute until smooth and slightly thickened. Stir in the beef suet until evenly distributed — you'll see little white flecks throughout, and that is exactly right.

Adding beef suet to a dessert is the most unhinged thing this recipe asks you to do and it is also the most correct thing. Suet was the original fat for this dish. Butter is a newcomer. We are honoring tradition and thriving.
4

Combine Everything — Gently

Add the soaked date mixture (all of it) directly into the wet egg and sugar bowl. Stir to combine. Then gently fold in the flour and spice mixture with a spatula until just combined — stop the moment you can't see dry flour anymore. The batter will look very loose. That is correct. Overmixing develops gluten and makes the sponge dense and chewy instead of light and tender.

Folding means gentle, sweeping motions from the bottom up. Not stirring. Not whisking. The batter should look slightly underdone when it goes in the pan — that's the point. Trust the process. Trust the dates.
5

Bake It

Preheat your oven to 175°C / 350°F. Pour the batter into your well-buttered baking dish and spread evenly. Bake for 35–45 minutes. Done when the top is set, the center springs back lightly, and a skewer inserted in the center comes out sticky from dates but not raw. The top should be a deep mahogany brown. Remove and let rest 5–10 minutes before adding sauce.

Every oven runs differently. Start checking at 33 minutes. If the top browns too fast, tent loosely with foil for the remaining time.
While the Pudding Bakes
04 The Toffee Sauce
1

Melt the Sugar into the Cream

In your medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream and dark brown sugar. Set over medium heat and stir frequently until the sugar is fully dissolved and the mixture is gently bubbling — about 4–5 minutes. Don't rush this with high heat or the sugar will seize.

This is the part of the recipe where your kitchen starts smelling like a caramel factory and you will immediately feel like a professional chef. Just let it happen. You've earned it.
2

Build the Gloss — Butter and Soy

Whisk in the cold cubed butter a few pieces at a time. Once incorporated, add the soy sauce. Simmer gently for 5–7 more minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Remove from heat. Stir in the vanilla bean paste and flaky sea salt. Taste it. Sweet, complex, slightly salty, deeply caramelized.

The sauce can be made up to 3 days ahead. Reheat gently in a saucepan over low heat, stirring. If it seems too thick, add a splash of cream and stir.
One tablespoon of soy sauce in a toffee dessert sauce. This is the most chaotic correct thing in modern British cooking. The Fallow chefs said "what if we just—" and the rest is culinary history. We honor it. We respect it.
The Grand Finale
05 Assembly — This Part is Critical
1

Poke, Pour, and Rest

While the pudding is still warm (5–10 minutes out of the oven), use a skewer to poke holes all over the surface — about 20–30 holes, evenly distributed. Pour one-third of the hot toffee sauce over the pudding and let it seep in. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes. The sauce soaks into the sponge, making the interior sticky and deeply saturated with toffee flavor in a way that just pouring sauce on top could never achieve.

If you pour all the sauce over before serving, you lose the experience of hot sauce on warm pudding. Keep ⅔ of the sauce back and pour it at the table. The presentation of sauce going over pudding in real time is part of the meal now.
2

Serve Immediately with Hot Sauce and Cold Cream

Cut generous portions and pour the remaining hot toffee sauce over each portion at the table — this is theater, this is the moment. Then add your cream element of choice. Serve immediately. This dessert does not wait.

The hot toffee sauce hitting cold clotted cream and melting it slightly while steam rises off the warm sponge — this is a cinematic moment. If no one films it for Instagram you are doing it wrong. This is content. This is dessert. These are not different things.
06 Serve With
Most Authentic
Clotted cream. The richness cuts through the sweetness and the cold-on-hot contrast is non-negotiable British pub behavior.
Modern Balance
Crème fraîche. The slight tang balances the sweetness and makes the dish feel a little more refined.
Most Traditional
Warm vanilla custard poured generously over the top. A second sauce situation. Controversial? Yes. Delicious? Absolutely non-negotiable.
Storage
Sponge keeps 3 days at room temp, covered. Sauce keeps 5 days refrigerated. Reheat both gently. Freezes for up to 2 months.

You Just Made a Pub Classic. 🍮

Sticky Toffee Pudding has been making people irrationally emotional in British pubs since the 1970s. And now you just made a version with Medjool dates, black treacle, beef suet, cardamom whispers, and a Michelin-brained toffee sauce that includes soy sauce — which sounds unhinged and tastes like destiny. Go eat it while it's warm. All decisions can wait.
